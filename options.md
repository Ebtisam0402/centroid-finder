JCodec (Pure Java):
Pros:
-Easy setup
-small and simple frame extractions and can read frames from video
-Written in java
cons:
-Doesn't support as many video format as FFMPEG
-May be slower for large and high quality videos
-Documentation can be limited or harder to follow
Velvet Video
Pros: I
-Includes video and audio processing future
-Can extract frames from video
-Support many video format like mp4, avi and web
Cons:
-Set up is complicated for begginners
-Harder to debuge
-Dependency down load can be large

JavaCV
Pros:
-Support many video formats including mp4.
-Active community support and documentation
-Can extract image frames from videos.
Cons:
-Setup can feel difficult at first.
-Dependencies are larger and may take time to download.
-Error messages can sometimes be difficult to understand.

OpenCV (Java Bindings)
Pros:
-Very powerful for image and video processing.
-Support many image and video processing features.
-Fast performance for processing frames.
Cons:
-Setup can be complicated for beginners.
-Documentation can be overwhelming because large library
-Maven setup may require additional steps.
